NSFAS Guide

Everything you need to apply, track and fix your NSFAS funding.

ℹ️

Always use the official NSFAS website only. Never pay anyone to apply — it's always free. my.nsfas.org.za →

How to Apply

✅ Step 1 — Check if you qualify
• SA citizen with valid ID ✅
• Household income below R350,000/year ✅
• Studying at a public university or TVET college ✅
• Not already funded by another full bursary ✅
🌐 Step 2 — Create your myNSFAS account
Go to my.nsfas.org.za → Click "Register" → Use your SA ID number → Use YOUR OWN email (not your parent's) → Create a strong password.
📄 Step 3 — Documents needed
• Your SA ID / Smart Card ✅
• Parent/guardian SA ID copies ✅
• Latest 3 payslips OR unemployment affidavit ✅
• SARS tax certificate (if applicable) ✅
• Your bank account details ✅
• Proof of registration (after university accepts you) ✅
📅 Step 4 — When to apply
Applications open every September/October for the following year. Apply the day applications open — late applications are almost never accepted.
⚠️ Common mistakes to avoid
• Wrong ID number ❌ • Incorrect bank details ❌
• Missing documents ❌ • Parent's email ❌
• Paying someone to apply ❌ (Always FREE!)
💳 Apply Now at myNSFAS →
🔍 How to check your status
1. Go to my.nsfas.org.za
2. Log in with your ID + password
3. Click "Track Funding Progress"
📊 What each status means
Submitted — Application received ✅
Under Review — Being assessed ⏳
Approved — You got funding! 🎉
Awaiting Registration — Register at varsity first
Rejected — Don't panic, you can appeal ❗
📱 Check via WhatsApp
Send "Hi" to NSFAS on WhatsApp: 082 090 0046
💰 2025 NSFAS Allowances
Living allowance: R1,500/month
Transport (far from campus): R7,000/year
Transport (near campus): R2,720/year
Meals: R1,500/month (if no meal plan)
Accommodation: Up to R45,000/year
Books & learning materials: R5,200/year
📅 When does money arrive?
Usually in the first 2 weeks of each month. Download the myNSFAS app to access your NSFAS Wallet.
⚠️

Many rejections are due to simple document errors. You CAN appeal!

📝 How to appeal
1. Log in to my.nsfas.org.za
2. Click "Submit Appeal"
3. Select the reason and write a clear explanation
4. Upload supporting documents
5. Submit and note your reference number
6. Wait 4–6 weeks for a decision
💸 Money not received?
1. Check bank details on myNSFAS are correct
2. Confirm you are fully registered at university
3. Call NSFAS: 0800 067 327 (free)
4. Visit your university's Financial Aid Office
🚨 NSFAS Scams
❌ Never pay anyone to apply — it's FREE
❌ Ignore WhatsApp messages promising "guaranteed NSFAS"
❌ Never share your myNSFAS password
✅ Only use my.nsfas.org.za

Tips
📝 Write a compelling motivation letter
Your motivation letter is your most important document. Be specific about your career goals, financial need, and why you deserve the bursary. 1–2 pages, professional, no spelling errors.
⏰ Apply early — every time
Most bursaries close before the end of the year for the following year. Set calendar reminders for August/September each year to check all bursary websites.
📋 Keep these documents ready always
• Certified SA ID copy
• Certified parent/guardian IDs
• Latest 3 parent payslips / proof of income
• Grade 12 results or latest academic transcript
• Proof of registration / acceptance letter
• Bank statement (3 months)
• CV (1 page)
🎯 Apply to 10+ bursaries at once
Don't rely on one application. Apply for every bursary you qualify for simultaneously. Most applications are free — the more you apply, the better your chances.
🚨 Bursary scam warning
❌ NEVER pay to apply for a bursary — all legitimate bursaries are FREE to apply
❌ Ignore Facebook/WhatsApp ads offering "easy bursaries for a fee"
✅ Only apply through official company or government websites

Accommodation

Find verified student accommodation near your campus.

🔒

Safety first! Always visit before paying. Never pay a deposit without a signed lease. If a deal sounds too good to be true — it's a scam.

Search on trusted platforms
🏠 StudentRoom.co.za — SA Student Specialist 🔍 Private Property — Verified Rentals 🏡 Property24 — All SA Properties 📋 Gumtree — Student Backrooms & Flats 🎓 Accommodation.ac.za — University Focused
Tips before you sign
📋 Always sign a lease agreement
A proper lease must include: rental amount, deposit, notice period, what's included (water, electricity, WiFi) and landlord contact details.
🔍 Visit the property first
Always inspect in person before paying. Check: safety of area, water pressure, electricity, locks, distance to campus transport.
💰 NSFAS accommodation allowance
NSFAS pays up to R45,000/year (R3,750/month). Your university must approve your residence first — always confirm with your Financial Aid Office!
🚨 Red flags — walk away!
❌ Landlord can't meet in person
❌ Price is unrealistically cheap
❌ Wants payment before you view
❌ No lease agreement offered
❌ WhatsApp only — can't call

🏫 Every university has free counselling
Every public university in SA has a Student Counselling and Wellness Centre. Services are FREE and confidential for registered students. Go to your university's website and search "Student Counselling" or "Student Wellness".
🤝 Higher Health Programme
Higher Health operates on all 26 public university campuses. They offer counselling, HIV testing, mental health support, and sexual health services — all free for students. Call 0800 36 36 36.
🔒 Counselling is confidential
Everything you share with a campus counsellor is private. They cannot tell your parents, your lecturers, or your friends. You are safe to speak openly.
😰 Financial stress is real — and common
More than 60% of SA students experience financial stress that affects their studies. You are not alone. There are resources to help.
🆘 Emergency help at your university
Most universities have emergency bursary funds for students facing sudden financial crises. Contact your university's Financial Aid Office immediately if you are struggling to eat, pay fees, or buy textbooks. Ask specifically about "emergency funding" or "hardship funds".
💳 NSFAS not arriving?
If your NSFAS payment is late, contact your university's Financial Aid Office first — they can often arrange emergency support while the issue is resolved. Call NSFAS: 0800 067 327
😴 Sleep is not a luxury — it's medicine
Consistently getting 7–8 hours of sleep improves memory, grades and mood. Create a regular sleep schedule even during exam time.
🚶 Move your body every day
Even a 20-minute walk reduces anxiety and improves focus. Many university campuses have free gyms for registered students. Use them!
📱 Take phone breaks
Social media comparison culture significantly increases anxiety and depression in students. Try a 1-hour phone-free period every evening.
🤝 Talk to someone you trust
Bottling up emotions makes everything harder. Reach out to a friend, a family member, or a counsellor when things feel heavy. You don't have to manage everything alone.
